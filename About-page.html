THE UNCLE ERNIE'S EXPERIENCE
Uncle Ernie's Bayfront Grill and Brew House, located in Historic St. Andrews, offers more than just fantastic food, excellent service, and an incredible view. Dining with us, you experience doses of history the moment you walk through our doors. Along with the friendly service, this place has become a top local's favorite and a must-see while you enjoy our award-winning food in the best relaxing atmosphere.
A RICH HISTORY IN ST. ANDREWS
Uncle Ernie's is the home of Ernest (Uncle Ernie) and Jessie Morris. The original house was built in the late 1800s, making it one of St. Andrews's oldest. Uncle Ernie was born May 19, 1893, in Point Washington, Florida. In 1910, he moved to St. Andrews, where he had four children with his first wife, Jessie. In 1923, he married Donald and Jessie Morris's niece Eunice Tunnell and had three more children. Uncle Ernie was a commercial fisherman, and the landmark building that is now Uncle Ernie's was home to many of his friends and family. Jim's wife, Doris, is remembered for her homemade biscuits and gravy. Uncle Ernie remained active in the community and lived in this home, for 23 years. We are honored and proud to keep this historic restaurant alive to share and honor Uncle Ernie's and Eunice's memories.
